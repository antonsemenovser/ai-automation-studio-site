How much does business automation cost in 2026?

Short answer: automating a single workflow starts around €120–€500, a connected multi-step system across several tools usually lands €500–€2,500, and company-wide automation runs higher. The real question isn't the price — it's how fast it pays for itself.

Last reviewed: June 2026 · based on freelance-marketplace rates and current build costs.

Quick price table

What you getTypical 2026 price
Single workflow — one trigger → one action (lead routing, reminders, order-to-sheet)€120–€500
Multi-step system — several tools connected, custom logic, notifications€500–€2,500
AI-powered automation — reads free-text messages, classifies, drafts replies€600–€3,000
Company-wide / mission-critical — high reliability, many integrations€3,000+

What drives the price

  • Number of tools to connect. One app talking to another is cheap; six systems that must stay in sync is not.
  • Messiness of the data. Clean, structured inputs are fast; free-text and inconsistent formats need extra parsing.
  • Custom logic or AI. Simple rules are cheap; understanding free-text messages or making decisions adds AI work.
  • How costly a failure is. A process that must never drop an order needs error-handling, retries and testing — that safety work is most of the price on serious builds.

Ongoing costs

  • Hosting: roughly €3–€10/month, sometimes less.
  • Third-party fees: some services charge per action or per message; an AI step can add a small per-message model cost.
  • Self-hosted / open tools: per-month cost can be near-zero.

How fast it pays for itself

Automation earns its price back in saved hours. If a task that someone does by hand every day gets automated, a €200–€500 build commonly pays back in one to three months — and keeps paying after that. The clearest wins are repetitive, rules-based jobs: data entry, copying between apps, reminders, status updates, report generation.

How to start cheaply

  1. Find the most repetitive task. The one a person does daily and dreads.
  2. Automate just that, end to end. A small automation that actually runs beats a grand system that's never finished.
  3. Measure the hours saved. That number justifies the next automation.
  4. Own the result. Get the source and run it under your own accounts.
