Do I need a bot or an app for my business?

Short answer: for most small and medium businesses, start with a bot — live in days, a fraction of the cost, no install. Build a native app only when you genuinely need offline use, device hardware, rich graphics, or a long-term platform with a large user base.

Last reviewed: June 2026.

Choose a bot when…

  • You want to launch fast and cheaply and prove demand.
  • The job is messaging, ordering, bookings, support, lead capture or notifications.
  • Your customers already use Telegram or WhatsApp.
  • You can't afford the drop-off of asking people to install an app.

Choose an app when…

  • It must work offline.
  • It relies on device hardware — camera, GPS, sensors — as its core.
  • It needs rich, real-time graphics (games, heavy interactive tools).
  • It's a long-term platform with a large, loyal user base that justifies the cost.

The honest middle path

You usually don't have to choose blindly. Start with a bot to validate demand for a small cost, learn what customers actually use, and earn while you learn. If real usage proves an app is justified, build it later with evidence instead of betting thousands on a guess.

FAQ

Bot or mobile app?
For most SMBs, a bot first — days to launch, a fraction of the cost, no install. App only for offline/device-hardware/graphics/large-platform needs.
Is a bot cheaper?
Yes, by a wide margin — hundreds of € and days, vs thousands and weeks–months for an app plus store fees and two-platform maintenance.
Why do bots convert better?
No install step — people open a bot in a chat app they already have instead of downloading an app for one business.
When is an app right?
Offline use, core device hardware, rich real-time graphics, or a long-term platform with a large user base.
Can I start with a bot and add an app later?
Yes — validate demand cheaply with a bot, then build an app with evidence if usage justifies it.
