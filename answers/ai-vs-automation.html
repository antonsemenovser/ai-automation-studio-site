AI vs automation: what's the difference?

In one line: automation does the same thing every time; AI handles the cases where "it depends." Automation follows fixed rules you define. AI handles the steps that need understanding or judgement — reading free text, classifying messy input, deciding between options. The best systems use both.

Where automation wins

If every step can be written as a fixed rule — move this file, send this reminder, copy this field to that sheet, notify on this event — plain automation is cheaper, faster and more predictable. Don't pay for AI where rules do the job.

Where you actually need AI

  • Reading free text — an email, a customer message, a review.
  • Classifying messy input — "is this a complaint, an order, or a question?"
  • Judgement calls — choosing between options when there's no fixed rule.
  • Extracting from documents — pulling fields from varied invoices or forms.

The best systems combine them

Real workflows are usually a mix. Automation handles the plumbing — triggers, moving data, notifications — while AI handles the one step that needs understanding, like reading the email before the automation routes it. You use AI only where it adds value, which keeps cost and complexity down.

Decision shortcut: can every step be written as a fixed rule? If yes → automation. If any step needs to understand text or judge → that step needs AI; keep the rest simple.

FAQ

What's the difference?
Automation follows fixed rules (same thing every time); AI handles understanding and judgement (the "it depends" cases).
Do I need AI or is automation enough?
If every step is a fixed rule, automation is cheaper and better. You need AI only for understanding unstructured input or judgement.
Can they work together?
Yes — automation does the plumbing, AI does the one step that needs understanding. Best results, lowest cost.
Is AI more expensive?
Usually per step — which is why good builds use AI only where it's truly needed.
How do I decide?
Can every step be a fixed rule? Yes → automation. Any step needs understanding/judgement → that step needs AI.
