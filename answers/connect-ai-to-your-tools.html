How to connect AI to your tools (integrations)

AI is most useful when it's wired into the tools you already use — CRM, spreadsheets, email, Telegram/WhatsApp, databases — so it can read a record, decide, and write back automatically. That connection is an integration, and it's what turns AI from "something you chat with" into "something that does the work."

Last reviewed: June 2026.

What an AI integration actually is

An integration is the connection that lets an AI model use your tools: read a record → understand it → decide → write back. Create a lead, update an order, send a reply, file a document — inside the systems you already run. You keep your tools; AI just gets hands.

What can be connected

CategoryExamples
CRMHubSpot, Pipedrive, and similar
SpreadsheetsGoogle Sheets, Excel
MessagingTelegram, WhatsApp, email
CommerceOnline stores, payment platforms
Anything with an APIDatabases, internal systems, SaaS tools

Rule of thumb: if a tool has an API or an export, it can usually be connected.

What it lets you automate

  • Lead → CRM. A message comes in; the AI qualifies it and creates the CRM record.
  • Order → sheet → notification. A new order flows to your sheet and pings your team.
  • Email → action. An incoming email is read, classified and acted on.
  • Data → alert. A change in a database triggers an AI-written summary to you.

You don't replace your tools

The point of an integration is to keep what you already use and add AI on top — your team's workflow stays familiar while the repetitive steps get automated. Replacing tools is rarely necessary and usually a mistake.

FAQ

Can AI connect to my current tools?
Yes — most tools (CRM, sheets, email, Telegram/WhatsApp, databases) expose an API or webhook an AI agent can read and write.
What is an AI integration?
The connection that lets AI use your tools — read a record, decide, write back — inside your existing workflow.
What tools can be connected?
CRMs, Google Sheets/Excel, email/calendar, Telegram/WhatsApp, commerce/payments, and anything with an API.
How much does it cost?
One tool: €150–€500. Several connected tools: €500–€2,000. Plus hosting and any service fees.
Do I replace my tools?
No — integrations keep your tools and add AI on top; replacing is rarely needed.
