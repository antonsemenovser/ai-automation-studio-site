AI agent for business: real use cases & cost

An AI agent earns its keep by taking one repetitive task off your team end to end — on your real data, with guardrails. A focused agent starts around €400–€1,500; multi-system agents run €1,500–€5,000+. Here are the use cases that actually pay back.

Last reviewed: June 2026.

Document & invoice processingReads, extracts, enters into your system, files

"How is this different from just using ChatGPT?"

A general chat tool answers when you ask it. A business AI agent is connected to your systems and runs your specific process automatically — on real data, with guardrails and logging, no copy-pasting prompts. It's the difference between a smart assistant you talk to and a worker that does the job.

How fast it pays back

If an agent takes over a task that eats several hours of staff time a week, the build commonly pays back in one to three months — and keeps saving after. The clearest wins are high-volume, rules-plus-judgement tasks done daily.

Where to start

Pick one painful, repetitive task with a clear input and output — refunds, lead qualification, invoice entry — and automate just that, end to end, with guardrails. A small agent that reliably runs beats an ambitious one that never ships, and it proves the value before you expand.
